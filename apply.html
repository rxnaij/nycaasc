What is NYCAASC?


The New York City Asian American Student Conference (NYCAASC) is a student run intercollegiate organization dedicated to addressing issues pertinent to Asian/Pacific/Americans on local, national, and global scales. Organized by a committee of representatives from New York University, Columbia University/Barnard College, CUNY, SUNY, and Fordham University, NYCAASC provides an accessible space every April for activists and student leaders to engage in dialogue on racial and sociopolitical matters. Featuring interactive workshops alongside local and nationally based speakers and performers, the conference is structured to encourage solidarity and initiative both within the community and beyond.

Why become a member of NYCAASC?

  • Be a part of a progressive, student run conference and find a community of like-minded, passionate individuals interested in social justice
  • Learn more about and become proactive on critical issues affecting the APA community
  • Get connected to an alumni network of community activists and professionals in the APA community and beyond
  • Gain a variety of skills and experiences in fields such as event planning, public relations, curriculum development, and more

Criteria

  • Must be a current high school, undergraduate, or graduate student in New York City
  • Must be available to attend the program on Sundays from 1-4pm
  • Prior experience in advocacy work or volunteering is preferable but not mandatory
  • Must have an interest in Asian/Pacific/American issues
  • You DO NOT have to identify as ethnically Asian or Asian American to apply
