High School Liason Program

Join the Spring 2016 High School Liaison Program

Hello there! Applications for the NYCAASC High School Liason Program are now closed. Please check back late Winter - early Spring for HSL Spring 2017.


Why become a High School Liaison member for NYCAASC?


As a HSL member, you will be able to see first-hand how a grassroots conference is put together. Throughout your time in this program, you will learn more about how different committees such as publicity, marketing, and workshop developmentâ€”function separately and in conjunction with one another. You will also learn more about Asian/Pacific American issues and identity, as well as develop leadership skills such as public speaking and facilitation.


What is expected of me as a High School Liaison member?

You will learn more about Asian/Pacific American histories, issues, and current events by:


  • Attending 12 weeks of 1-hour workshops, followed by planning meetings at New York University
  • Participating in a specific NYCAASC committee to learning how a conference is organized
  • Attending workshops on the day of the conference, Saturday, April 23, 2015
  • Assisting conference organizers as needed on the day of NYCAASC

Criteria

  • Must be currently attending a New York City high school and be in good academic standing
  • Must be available to attend the program on Sundays from 1-4pm
  • Prior experience in advocacy work or volunteering is preferable but not mandatory
  • Must have an interest in Asian/Pacific/American issues
  • You DO NOT have to identify as ethnically Asian or Asian American to apply

If you are interested in becoming a part of NYCAASC, please complete the application below. Interviews may be requested. Feel free to email any questions or concerns you may have to hsl@nycaasc.com.
