NYCAASC: ACT: Empowering Our Generation

This April, the New York City Asian American Student Conference celebrates its 3rd Anniversary with ACT: Empowering Our Generation.

Join us on 4/18 at the NYU Kimmel Center to discuss A/P/A issues and reflect upon our history, action and inactions.

Keynote Speaker + Performances

Fay Chiang, Keynote Speaker

Fay Chiang has been a poet, visual artist, community and cultural activist in NYC Chinatown and the Lower East Side for the past 39 years. She was former executive director of the Basement Workshop (the first Asian American nonprofit multidisciplinary cultural organization in NYC and the east coast from 1971 to 1986), and director of Henry Street Settlement’s Asian American Outreach Program. She joined Project Reach in 2000 and is currently working in program development, crisis counseling for young people at risk and supporting youth and adults living with HIV/AIDS.

As the author of two volumes of poetry, In The City of Contradictions, and Miwa’s Song, her poetry and prose have been published nationally and internationally in numerous poetry anthologies and literary magazines. A recipient of a New York State CAPS Poetry grant, a Revson Fellowship at Columbia University, a Lifetime Achievement Award from New York University’s Asian/Pacific American Studies Department, and The Five Colleges, MA, Fay has taught poetry, visual arts and playwriting as an artist in residence in a variety of venues.

A fine arts student at Hunter College of CUNY, she received a Bachelor of Arts from the School of Visual Arts in New York City. As a Revson Fellow at Columbia University, she studied scriptwriting and film history. In 2003-2004, she was the artist in residence at New York University’s Asian/Pacific/American Studies program, where she worked on her book-length poem “Chinatown”, and participated in classroom discussions and public programming. In 2004-2007 as a visiting scholar under the auspices of NYU/APA’s program, she continued her research/work on “Chinatown”, and finished a book-length poetry manuscript, “In This Life”, based on her conversations as a breast cancer survivor with a friend living with AIDS.

A volunteer for Zero Capital, Pine Ridge Project, Orchard Street Arts and Advocacy, October 22nd Coalition Against Police Brutality, and a board member of non profits: BorderStatements, When I Walk, and Dramatic Risks, she continues her battle with her sixth bout of breast cancer. Fay lives in the East Village and is mother of the inimitable Xian. She is currently working on a memoir, a one act play, and a children’s illustrated story book.
