Track 1: Within

Interrogating Asian Identity Within U.S. Settler Colonialism


Description

Through this presentation Kat works to situate "Asian American" identity within a context that identifies, defines, examines, and interrogates what it means to be Asian American within the structures of U.S. specific settler colonialism and how those implications affect our relationship to resistance movements in the U.S.

Participants will move their understandings of settler colonialism as something that happened at a fix point in the past into an understanding of how settler colonialism is a structure that shapes the entire fabric of the society we live in and how we see ourselves in it. Kat has organized a series of lessons based around concepts specifically presented in a way that will help participants to recontextualize the world around them as well as their own identities. Together we will disrupt deeply ingrained narratives that we live our lives through that uphold and perpetuate oppressive and violent systems.
