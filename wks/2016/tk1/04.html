Track 1: Within

Cracking the Bamboo Ceiling


Description

WThis workshop presentation and panel will analyze the “Bamboo Ceiling” on different career options facing the Asian American community. How can qualified Asian-Americans who demonstrate strong leadership potential and specialized skills rise above their talent? Are there certain behaviors caused by the construction of this Bamboo Ceiling? How can we, as individuals and collectively as a society, rise above the Bamboo Ceiling?

There are many checkpoints in which an Asian American has to go through in order to become successful in a predominantly white culture. Whether the first step be that your parents/grandparents left their home country to strive for a better life in a new nation, or be that you left your native homeland to receive a better education, it is not easy to move through checkpoints in life. Many Asian Americans are now striving for college-degrees in America and entering the competing workforce. The Bamboo Ceiling is a checkpoint for those who want to succeed to higher positions in their respective careers.

This workshop will discuss Asian Americans representation in varying job fields, and will feature: Dr. Arun Rao, Senior Software Engineer at Pixar Animation Studios, and Connie Li, Court Clerk at Queens County Supreme Court.
