Track 2: Around

South and Southeast Asians: Excommunicated or Uncommunicative?


Description

In the A/P/A community, focus has historically been on the East Asian members of the diaspora. South and Southeast Asian groups eventually joined the fold, and established themselves abroad as well. But even now, these groups are frequently pushed out from discussions in the A/P/A community, due to perceived lacks of commonality or other factors. This workshop, facilitated by the Indonesian and Bengali Student Associations at NYU, will serve as a panel to discuss how South and Southeast Asians fit into the larger community of A/P/A. Audience members will listen to the panelists discuss the issues through the guiding questions posed by Shashank Rao (the moderator), and will have opportunities to contribute to the discussion.
