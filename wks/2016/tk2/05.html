Track 2: Around

Lunchroom Clashes and Food for Thought


Description

Food is often a central part of people’s lives, bringing family, friends, and others together. However, in the context of immigration, situations where food is involved can sometimes invite criticism and even aggression. In this workshop, Dr. Krishnendu Ray will be working with APA BRIDGE to explore the place of food in the A/P/A community, and how it shapes relations with others. Places of discussion will include school, the workplace, and restaurants. Dr. Ray will discuss the evolution and place of Asian ethnic cuisines in America, and how that pertains to the development of the Asian American identity and psyche. Then, the discussion will become more free form, guided by questions issued by APA Bridge, at which point participants can also contribute.
