Track 2: Around


Nate Bae Kupel


Nate Bae Kupel is the creator of the Transracial Korean Adoptee Nexus blog, founder of Aubra Consulting (an audio-branding consulting service), and former President of Boston Korean Adoptees, Inc., an adult Korean adoptee non-profit. He is also an award-winning radio producer for a national Asian American public radio pilot, As I Am: Asians in America hosted by Helen Zia. He has published several works including “Asian Adoptees and Post-Adoption Services in Massachusetts: Data from Providers and Reflections from Adult Adoptees,” and a profile on Korean Americans in Massachusetts, through the Institute for Asian American Studies at Umass Boston. He also co-authored a survey report of the 2010 International Korean Adoptee Association Gathering, in Seoul, South Korea. He is currently a Senior Consultant with Crescendo Consulting Group (CCG). CCG provides professional services to organizations in healthcare and other non-profit/for-profit organizations.

He received his Masters degree in Clinical Social Work at Simmons College, in Boston, MA, and a Bachelors of Arts in Sociology and certificate in Asian American Studies at the UMass Amherst.
