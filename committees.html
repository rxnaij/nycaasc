Committees

NYCAASC consists of 6 core committees. Read below to learn more about membership opportunities.

Events Committee


The Events Committee is responsible for organizing the performances and the vendors that support the conference. Events committee members assist with finding artists and performers for the conference and arranging fundraising events. On the day of the conference, the committee is in charge of managing performers as well as catering and the conference lunch.


High School Liaison Committee


Read more on the HSL Program page.


Logistics Committee


Logistics Committee members handle the behind-the-scenes work of the conference. By working with Kimmel Center facilitators, NYCAASC committee members, and outside organizations, the logistics committee organizes the conference in order to streamline the process during the planning stages as well ensure all runs smoothly the day of.


Marketing Committee


The Marketing committee supports the promotional and branding needs of the organization by ensuring all public content is consistent with NYCAASC’s mission and brand. The committee works with the Webmaster and Graphics Designer to create print materials, place web content, and determine designs for other promotional materials such as t-shirts, mugs, and buttons for the conference. Additionally, the Marketing committee spearheads the development of the conference program and provides a press kit for public release.


Publicity Committee


The Publicity Committee spreads public awareness for NYCAASC through media outreach and interpersonal relationships. The publicity committee is the primary contact for fundraising and negotiating co-sponsorships. Additionally, the Publicity Committee works closely with the Marketing Committee to generate social media campaigns.


Workshops Committee


The Workshops Committtee is responsible for generating much of the conference’s educational content. Normally, there are three tracks with a varying number of workshops per track. Members will coordinate specific workshops, think of potential speakers and topics, and run point on their respective workshops on the day-of.
